# Combat Writing Techniques

## Show vs Tell Power

- **Don't**: "He was very strong"
- **Show**: The shockwave shattered a cliff a hundred meters away. The opponent's protective golden light cracked like an eggshell, fissures spreading through the void.

## Power Level Escalation
1. Basic attacks → Impact sounds, light flashes, shockwaves
2. Mid-level → Terrain changes, weather anomalies
3. Top-level → Law manifestation, space-time distortion
4. Transcendent → Unwatchable, incomprehensible, only felt

## Camera Language
1. Wide shot → Battle panorama, positioning, environmental damage
2. Medium shot → Action interaction, clash of techniques
3. Close-up → Facial details, gritted teeth, sweat, eyes
4. Extreme close-up → Slow-motion (0.5s stretched to 5 sentences)
5. God's eye → Spectator reactions, celestial anomalies

## Pacing Structure
```
Setup (10%)  : Probing → Psychological game
Build (30%)  : Minor clashes → Escalation
Turn (40%)  : Aces revealed → Crisis
Resolution (20%)  : Comeback → Aftermath
```

## Fight Dialogue
- During fight → Short, fragmented, exclamation marks and ellipses
- Standoff → Psychological warfare, information play
- Aftermath → Long dialogue revealing motives/worldbuilding
